Practical Data Analytics and Mining

Course Description

The unit will teach the students how to effectively use databases for decision support. The unit has two main components. First, the students will learn how to design a data warehouse. This will provide hands-on experience on modelling and implementing a data warehouse. Second, the students will learn data mining techniques; how they work and how to use them. Also the students will have hands-on experience using data mining toolkits, including how to visualise and interpret the results.

Books

Key Texts: Golfarelli, M S. Rizzi, Data Warehouse Design: Modern Principles and Methodologies, McGraw Hill, 2009, ISBN 978-0-07-161039-1. Du, H. (2010). Data Mining Techniques and Applications: An Introduction. Cengage Learning. (ISBN 978-1- 84480-891- 5)

Syllabus

  1. Introduction to data warehouses and star schema.
  2. Data warehouse modelling and implementation.
  3. Extraction, transformation and loading.
  4. Data mining concepts and related areas.
  5. Data classification techniques.
  6. Data clustering techniques.
  7. Association rule mining.
  8. Time series analysis.
  9. Mining data streams.
  10. Emerging topics in data mining.

Assessmet Schedule and Strategy

1. The students will be given a structured coursework that will be delivered at different stages over the unit Students will demonstrate their ability by carrying out tasks such as designing and implementing a data warehouse using an industry standard tool. For the Data mining section, the students will use Weka for comparing the performance between different algorithms. The coursework will contain a research component where the student will write a short literature review. Overall word limit for the coursework is 4000 words max.
